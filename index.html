The Math of Love

By Josophat Makawa

This is not a test.

It is a short story about models, assumptions, and what happens when they fail.

In mathematics, we begin with assumptions.

Not because they are true, but because they make reasoning possible.

Three assumptions were adopted:

  • Logic explains behavior.
  • Attention follows predictable rules.
  • Emotion can be ignored without consequence.

A model was constructed.

It described focus as a function of proximity, interest as a measurable variable, and detachment as a stable state.

\[f(x) = k \cdot \frac{1}{d(x, \text{you})}\]

On paper, the model was elegant.

The original hypothesis was reconsidered:

Individuals oriented toward logic are unaffected by emotion.

If this were true, the observed data would be impossible.

And yet, the data persisted.

The hypothesis was false.

Not because the math was wrong, but because it was incomplete.

Some variables are not errors.

They are the point.

And in this model, there was only one.

You.
